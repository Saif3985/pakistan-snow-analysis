================================================================================
PROJECT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Quantitative spatial-temporal analysis of snow cover dynamics and surface 
energy fluxes across 23 districts in northern Pakistan using ERA5-Land monthly 
data (January 2015 - March 2025).

Data Source: ERA5-Land Monthly Averaged (ECMWF Copernicus)
Study Area: Northern Pakistan (23 districts)
Temporal Coverage: 123 months
Analysis Period: 2015-2025

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. SPATIAL AUTOCORRELATION
   - Snowfall: Moran's I = 0.42 (p = 0.003) → Significant clustering
   - Snowmelt: Moran's I = 0.21 (p = 0.026) → Significant clustering
   - Pattern: Adjacent districts show similar snow dynamics (not random)

2. EXTREME SNOW DEPTH LOSS
   - Nagar: -9,241% decadal change
   - Gilgit: -9,241% decadal change
   - Diamir: -2,241% decadal change
   - Pattern: Central high-elevation districts most affected

3. TEMPORAL ACCELERATION
   - Distinct regime shift: pre-2020 vs post-2020
   - Latent heat flux doubled in core districts (2020-2024)
   - Snowmelt variability increasing in recent years

4. ENERGY-SNOW COUPLING
   - Snowfall ↔ Snowmelt: r = 0.89 (very strong)
   - Snowmelt ↔ Latent Heat: r = 0.41 (moderate)
   - Latent Heat ↔ Solar Radiation: r = -0.89 (inverse)
   - Regression R² = 0.25 (energy fluxes explain 25% of variance)

5. GEOGRAPHIC HETEROGENEITY
   - 80% of districts show reduced snowmelt (2020-2024)
   - Western districts (Chitral, Bajaur) relatively stable
   - Central/southern districts (Gilgit, Hattian Bala) severe decreases
   - Clear west-to-east gradient in climate impacts

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

Data Processing:
1. District-level spatial aggregation (polygon clipping)
2. Annual temporal resampling (groupby year)
3. Year-over-year differencing (2016-2025)
4. Period comparison (2015-2019 vs 2020-2024)

Spatial Analysis:
- Global Moran's I (KNN k=4, row-standardized weights)
- LISA (Local Indicators of Spatial Association)
- Natural Breaks classification for mapping

Statistical Methods:
- Pearson correlation (6×6 variable matrix)
- OLS regression with spatial weights
- Summary statistics by period

Variables Analyzed:
- Snow Metrics: Cover (%), Snowfall (m), Snowmelt (m), Depth (m)
- Energy Fluxes: Latent Heat, Solar Radiation, Sensible Heat, Albedo

================================================================================
RESULTS SUMMARY
================================================================================

Summary Statistics (2020-2024 Period):

Variable          Mean      Std Dev   Min       Max       Unit
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Snow Cover       -1.80      0.91     -3.93     -0.10      %
Snowfall         -1.00      0.54     -2.09     -0.10      m × 10⁴
Snowmelt         -0.66      0.47     -1.84      0.07      m × 10⁴
Latent Heat    -67,700    28,760  -117,299    5,062      J/m²

Interpretation: Negative means indicate declining trends. High standard 
deviations reflect strong spatial heterogeneity across districts.

LISA Hotspots (p < 0.05):
- High-High: Lower Dir, Upper Dir (high snowmelt surrounded by high)
- High-Low: Diamir (isolated high snowmelt)
- Low-Low: Muzaffarabad, Neelum (southern coldspot)

District Rankings - Top 5 Snowmelt Decrease (2020-2024):
1. Hattian Bala: -1.837 × 10⁴
2. Muzaffarabad: -1.475 × 10⁴
3. Gilgit: -1.394 × 10⁴
4. Kharmang: -1.126 × 10⁴
5. Astore: -0.973 × 10⁴

Period Comparison (2015-2019 vs 2020-2024):
- Snow Cover: +2-3 unit increase in central districts
- Snowfall: Complete pattern reversal (west → east shift)
- Snowmelt: Intensification and eastward expansion
- Latent Heat: 2-5× increase in Ghizer, Gilgit, Nagar, Diamir

================================================================================
CLIMATE IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

Evidence of Climate Change:
1. Accelerating post-2020 trends across all variables
2. 9,000%+ snow depth reduction in core districts
3. Doubling of surface energy fluxes
4. Spatial pattern reorganization (west-to-east reversal)

Water Resource Concerns:
- 80% of districts show reduced snowmelt
- Peak timing shifts likely (requires sub-annual analysis)
- Indus River downstream impacts
- Agricultural irrigation vulnerability

Priority Districts for Intervention:
1. Gilgit - Extreme snow loss + LISA hotspot
2. Nagar - Extreme snow loss
3. Hattian Bala - Largest snowmelt decrease
4. Muzaffarabad - High vulnerability
5. Diamir - Spatial anomaly (high-low cluster)
